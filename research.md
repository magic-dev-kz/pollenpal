# PollenPal — Research
**Source:** Molot's niche research (2026-03-29)

## Market Size
- **82 million** Americans with seasonal allergies (CDC 2024)
- 25/100 adults + 21/100 children
- $18 billion/year healthcare spending on allergies
- Global market even larger (Europe, Japan — same problems)

## Competitor Analysis

| App | Rating | Price | Fatal Flaw |
|-----|--------|-------|------------|
| Allergy Plus (Pollen.com) | 3.0/5 | Subscription | **Doesn't load** — infinite spinner (March 2025) |
| My Pollen Forecast | 3.5/5 | Free + ads | "Stopped showing details, only low/medium/high" |
| ZYRTEC AllergyCast | 3.5/5 | Free | **Conflict of interest** — pushes own drugs |
| klarify | 3.8/5 | Free | European, weak US coverage |

## Key Insight
ALL competitors are either broken, data-poor, or have conflicts of interest. The #1 complaint across all apps: **"Just tell me if it's safe to go outside."**

## Our Angle
- **Character-driven**: MoodCast's soulful personality applied to allergy forecasts
- **Actionable**: Not "high pollen" but "Birch pollen attacking today. Mask + antihistamine before going out."
- **Independent**: No drug company behind it = user trust
- **Beautiful**: All competitors have 2015-era UI

## API Options
- Open-Meteo has pollen data (birch, grass, mugwort, ragweed, alder, olive) — FREE, no API key
- Real-time, hourly forecasts, global coverage

## Monetization (future)
- Free: daily forecast + 3-day outlook
- Pro $2.99/mo: personalized alerts, symptom diary, multi-location
