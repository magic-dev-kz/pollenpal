# PollenPal — Marketing Package
**Author:** Igor (Marketing)
**Date:** 2026-03-29

---

## 1. Naming

**App Name:** PollenPal - Allergy Forecast
- 28 characters (within 30 limit)
- "PollenPal" = friendly, personal, memorable
- "Allergy Forecast" = instant clarity on what the app does
- SEO bonus: hits "allergy" and "forecast" in title

Alternative considered: "PollenPal - Pollen Tracker" — weaker because "tracker" implies logging, not predicting.

---

## 2. Tagline / Slogan

**Main:** "Know before you sneeze."

Backup options:
- "Is it safe to go outside? Now you know."
- "Your child's allergy bodyguard."
- "Stop guessing. Start breathing."

Rationale: "Know before you sneeze" is short, rhythmic, slightly playful, and captures the core value — prediction, not reaction. Works for ads, screenshots, social.

## 3. App Store Description

### Short Description (80 chars)
Real-time pollen forecast. Know when it's safe to go outside.

### Full Description

```
Your child has allergies. Every morning you wonder: is it safe to go outside today?

PollenPal gives you a clear Safety Score from 1 to 10, updated hourly.
Birch, grass, ragweed — see exactly what's in the air and get specific advice:
when to walk, when to stay in, when to take antihistamines.

No drug company behind it. No broken data. No ads. Just honest answers.

WHAT YOU GET:
- Safety Score (1-10) — one glance, instant decision
- 6 pollen types tracked: birch, grass, ragweed, mugwort, alder, olive
- 3-day forecast — plan the weekend without anxiety
- Personal advice — not "high pollen" but "take antihistamine 30 min before going out"
- Beautiful design that doesn't look like a medical app from 2015

WHY POLLENPAL:
Allergy Plus charges $4.99 and shows an infinite spinner.
ZYRTEC's free app pushes their own drugs.
We built PollenPal because our kids have allergies too.

Free. No account required. No tracking. Just clean air data.
```

**Key principle:** First 3 lines visible without "more" — they must sell. The rest confirms.

## 8. Viral Potential

### Share Moment: Safety Score Screenshot
**When:** User sees a great score (8+) or terrible score (2-)
**What:** Beautiful card with score + city + date + "via PollenPal"
**Format:** Instagram story / WhatsApp share
**Copy:** "PollenPal says it's safe today! Park time!" or "PollenPal says STAY INSIDE. Score: 1.3"

**Viral mechanic:** Moms share safety info with other moms. It's not bragging — it's caring. Natural share behavior.

### Widget
**iOS widget (medium):** Safety Score + today's dominant pollen + one-line advice
**Value:** Mom checks widget 3-5x/day. Never needs to open the app. But the widget IS the ad — everyone who sees her phone sees PollenPal.

### TikTok / Reels Potential
- "POV: You're a mom checking if it's safe to take your allergic kid outside" — showing the app
- "Things that changed my spring as an allergy mom" — listicle format
- Seasonal content: "Birch season just started. Here's what you need to know."
- Before/after: hospital visit vs. PollenPal check = no incident

### Organic Growth Channels
- Mom communities (Facebook groups, Reddit r/Allergies, r/Parenting)
- Allergy season IS marketing season (March-September in Northern Hemisphere)
- Product Hunt launch for design community angle
- Pediatricians' offices — QR code flyer

## 9. Monetization — Freemium Model

### Free Tier (forever free)
- Daily Safety Score
- 6 pollen types with current levels
- 3-day forecast
- Character advice
- Light/dark theme
- One saved location

### Pro Tier — $2.99/month or $19.99/year
- Morning alerts (custom time, push notification with score)
- Symptom diary (track how you feel vs. pollen levels over time)
- Multiple locations (track home + school + grandma's house)
- Historical data (see patterns: "Birch peaks in your area around April 15")
- Widget customization
- Priority data refresh (every 30 min vs. every 60 min)

### Pricing Rationale
- Allergy Plus charges $4.99/mo and is broken = our $2.99 feels like a steal
- Annual at $19.99 = 2 months free, encourages commitment before allergy season
- Trial: 7-day free trial of Pro (ask after 3 days of use, AFTER a positive moment)

### Revenue Projection (conservative)
- 10K downloads in first allergy season
- 5% conversion to Pro = 500 subscribers
- 500 x $2.99 = ~$1,500/month = ~$18,000/year
- Scales with seasons and word-of-mouth
